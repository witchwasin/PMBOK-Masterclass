## A. Concept Check

Expected answer: Process Groups are management functions; Project Phases are lifecycle/delivery segments. Example: an ERP `Build and Migration` phase can include Planning, Executing and Monitoring & Controlling at the same time.

## B. ERP Scenario Question

Strong answer identifies multiple active Process Groups:

- Executing: SI prototype/build activity may be underway
- Monitoring & Controlling: data readiness, UAT capacity and migration evidence must be measured
- Planning: data workstream, cleansing rules, retest and gate criteria may need replanning
- Initiating/Sponsor escalation: required if charter-level objective, go-live constraint or major baseline is affected
- Closing: transition risk increases if ownership is unresolved

Answer should identify evidence that exists and evidence that is missing, then separate work that can continue from work that should pause or replan.

## C. Hotel Booking Decision Case

Payment failure and low conversion are Monitoring & Controlling signals from beta. Corrective action may include fixing checkout defects, simplifying flow, adding payment reliability work and revising release criteria. Replanning is needed if launch readiness, scope, schedule or risk baseline is affected.

Sponsor/Product Owner decision should consider customer impact, payment reliability, brand risk, high-season timing, support readiness and whether a limited launch is safer than full launch.

## D. Artifact Construction

Minimum usable Lifecycle and Process Group Map includes:

- Project boundary and transition point from Lesson 02
- lifecycle phases that are not named only after Process Groups
- exit evidence and decision gate for each phase
- management activities mapped to Process Groups
- feedback signals and replanning triggers
- closing evidence and operation owner
- approval fields

Professional maps include owner, evidence update, threshold, open-item transfer and benefits review date.

## E. Artifact Review

The flawed statement has at least these errors:

- Treats Process Groups as phases
- Places Monitoring after build is complete
- Ignores Executing and Monitoring & Controlling overlap
- Makes Closing only a lessons learned meeting
- Omits replanning and change control
- Omits transition to operation
- Omits acceptance evidence and decision gates

A corrected flow should show lifecycle phases such as Discovery/Blueprint, Build, Test, Launch/Cutover and Hypercare, with Process Groups mapped across them and feedback loops inside delivery.

## F. Reflection

Strong reflection names one weak Process Group and explains the consequence in evidence, decision or value terms. For example, weak Closing creates unresolved owner and benefit-review gaps even when output is delivered.
