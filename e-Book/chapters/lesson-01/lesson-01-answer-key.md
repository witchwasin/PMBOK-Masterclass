## A. Concept Check

A strong answer says PMBOK is a body of knowledge and shared professional language. A methodology is a chosen way of working in a specific organization or project. A template is an artifact format. PMBOK may inform methodology and templates, but it is not identical to either.

## B. Scenario Question

ERP go-live is output evidence, not full success evidence. If Finance still closes in 15 days, the target outcome has not been achieved. A strong status report should separate:

- Output: ERP deployed, data migrated, integrations/training completed if true
- Outcome gap: monthly close has not improved to the 5-working-day target
- Possible causes: adoption, process ownership, data quality, unresolved defects or unclear operating accountability
- Owner: CFO or Finance Process Owner for close-cycle outcome, with PM coordinating recovery evidence during transition
- Next evidence: adoption data, close-cycle measure, issue log, process owner decision and benefits review date

## C. Decision Case

Strong questions before choosing approach:

- Which parts of scope are stable and which require discovery
- Which regulatory or finance controls cannot be relaxed
- What is the cost of missing the 1 October go-live constraint
- How available are the 80 key users for iterative feedback
- What decisions require Sponsor or Steering Committee approval
- Which outputs need formal acceptance before dependent work starts
- What team capability exists for Agile, Predictive or Hybrid delivery

Recommended starting approach may be Hybrid: predictive governance for procurement, compliance, data migration, cutover and finance controls; iterative cycles for selected design feedback, user adoption experiments or workflow refinement. Predictive can also be defensible if the learner states evidence that scope is stable, compliance dominates and feedback windows are constrained.

The answer must include decision owner, evidence, trade-off and review trigger. “Agile is faster” is insufficient because speed is not evidence of fit, risk control, stakeholder availability or decision authority.

## D. Artifact Construction

Minimum usable PM Learning Baseline:

- Business Need appears before solution
- Output, outcome, benefit and value are separated
- Benefit owner is named and not assigned only to PM by default
- Blind spots include at least two concrete limits in current thinking
- Evidence gaps are written as questions or validation actions
- Scenario facts do not contradict source registers
- Approval section names creator, owner, reviewer, approval authority and evidence

Professional answers connect assumptions to scenario evidence and identify how Lesson 02 will validate project/operation boundary and value chain.

## E. Artifact Review

The statement “Hotel Booking mobile app and website launch on time in the 12 ล้านบาท budget, therefore project success” is incomplete because it proves only delivery performance and selected outputs.

Missing evidence may include:

- Booking completion rate or conversion evidence
- Direct booking movement from 10% toward 35%
- PMS inventory sync reliability
- Payment and booking confirmation success rate
- Back office adoption by hotel staff
- Customer support readiness
- Benefit owner and review date after launch

A stronger rewritten statement separates output, outcome, benefit and value:

The platform launched within the Phase 1 budget. This confirms output delivery, but success must also be reviewed through booking completion, direct booking growth, reduced OTA commission, operational adoption and customer experience metrics over the agreed post-launch period.

## F. Reflection

A strong reflection respects experience while explaining that shared framework reduces blind spots and makes decisions transferable. It should say tailoring prevents unnecessary process by adjusting controls to risk, uncertainty and decision needs rather than treating PMBOK as a checklist.
