# Instructor Guide — Chapter 01: ทำไม Project Manager ต้องรู้ PMBOK

## Teaching Notes

บทนี้เป็นบทตั้งฐานคิด ไม่ใช่บทให้ผู้เรียนจำ process หรือ artifact จำนวนมาก จุดประสงค์คือทำให้ผู้เรียนหยุดมอง Project Management เป็น “การตามงาน” และเริ่มมองเป็นการเชื่อม Business Need ไปสู่ Business Value ผ่าน evidence, ownership และ decision

ลิงก์ไป Learner Chapter: [lesson-01-learner.md](./lesson-01-learner.md)

## Learning Intent

หลังจบบท ผู้เรียนควรเปลี่ยนจากคำถามว่า “ต้องใช้ template ไหน” ไปเป็น “ต้องตัดสินใจอะไร ใครเป็น owner และ evidence อะไรพอให้ตัดสินใจ”

บทนี้ต้องล็อกความเข้าใจ 4 เรื่อง:

1. PMBOK เป็น Body of Knowledge ไม่ใช่ methodology
2. ประสบการณ์กับ framework ต้องทำงานร่วมกัน
3. Output, Outcome, Benefit และ Business Value ต้องแยกจากกัน
4. Tailoring ต้องมีเหตุผล ไม่ใช่ตัด control เพื่อความเร็ว

## Thinking Walkthrough

### Step 1: Start from Business Need

For ERP, do not start with “install ERP.” Start with the business problem: fragmented data and monthly close taking 15 days. The target is to close within 5 working days. This forces the learner to separate the requested solution from the problem being solved.

### Step 2: Separate Output from Outcome

Output is what the project team can deliver directly: configured ERP modules, migrated data, integrations, training, cutover and support plan.

Outcome requires business adoption: Finance and related teams actually use the new process and data so the close cycle improves.

### Step 3: Find the Owner

The learner often names the PM as owner of everything. Coach them to distinguish artifact ownership from benefit ownership. PM may own coordination and evidence before transition, but post-handover benefit ownership should sit with the business owner, such as CFO or Finance Process Owner in the ERP case.

### Step 4: Surface Blind Spots

Ask: “If your background is technical, what might you underweight?” Common answers are adoption, process ownership, key-user capacity, data quality, acceptance criteria and benefits review.

### Step 5: Convert Gaps into Learning Actions

The PM Learning Baseline is usable only when gaps become questions or validation actions. “Users may resist” is less useful than “Need adoption baseline, key-user capacity plan and owner for post-go-live usage measure.”

## Completed Example

### Context

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| Business Need | Reduce monthly close time and create trustworthy shared enterprise data |
| Assumed Output | ERP 5 modules, migrated data, integrations, training, cutover and support plan |
| Expected Outcome | Finance uses standardized process and shared data so monthly close reaches 5 working days |
| Benefit Owner | CFO or Finance Process Owner after handover |

### Current Mental Model

The learner initially thought PM success meant go-live on time and within budget. This proves delivery performance but does not prove adoption, outcome or benefit.

### Blind Spots

- No adoption baseline yet
- Key-user capacity for design and UAT is uncertain
- Data ownership across 6 legacy systems is unclear
- Benefits review date is not defined
- Post-handover owner has not accepted accountability

### Learning Actions

Lesson 02 should use this baseline to classify project vs operation and map value from business need to output, outcome, benefit and transition ownership.

## Model Answer

A professional response identifies ERP installation as output and the reduction of monthly close from 15 days to 5 working days as outcome/benefit that requires process adoption, data quality and business ownership.

Minimum questions before declaring success:

- What root causes make monthly close take 15 days now
- Who owns Finance process and data after go-live
- What adoption baseline exists for affected users
- What capacity do 80 key users have for requirements, testing and training
- What acceptance criteria prove the process works
- When will benefits review happen after go-live

The artifact owner is the learner, but the scenario benefit owner should be CFO or Finance Process Owner, not the PM alone. The PM ensures ownership and evidence are agreed before transition.

Other answers are acceptable when they distinguish fact from assumption and propose a way to verify uncertain claims.

## Review Checklist

- [ ] Business Need appears before solution
- [ ] Output, Outcome, Benefit and Business Value are separated
- [ ] Benefit Owner after handover is identified
- [ ] At least two blind spots are named
- [ ] Evidence gaps include validation actions
- [ ] PMBOK is not described as a fixed methodology
- [ ] Go-live is not treated as full success by itself
- [ ] ERP working budget and total funding envelope are not confused
- [ ] Hotel Booking budget and direct booking target are not changed

## Scoring Rubric

| Dimension | Incomplete | Usable | Professional |
|---|---|---|---|
| Business Need | Starts from solution | States the business problem | Connects baseline, target and evidence |
| Value Chain | Mixes output and outcome | Separates output/outcome/benefit | Connects benefit owner and review date |
| Blind Spots | Names none | Names limits in current thinking | Converts limits into validation questions |
| Governance | No owner | Names at least one owner | Separates creator, owner, reviewer and approver |
| Scenario Accuracy | Contradicts source facts | Uses key facts correctly | Uses facts correctly and labels assumptions |
| Tailoring Logic | Chooses method by preference | Gives contextual reason | States evidence, trade-off and review trigger |

Passing level: every dimension at least Usable. Professional level: mostly Professional with no scenario contradiction.

## Debrief Questions

- Which sentence in your answer proves you are not treating PMBOK as a checklist
- Where did you separate output from outcome most clearly
- Which blind spot would be most dangerous in ERP
- Which Hotel Booking metric would prove value rather than only delivery
- What evidence would change your delivery approach recommendation

## Common Learner Errors

- Calling ERP go-live the outcome
- Naming the PM as permanent owner of business benefits
- Treating “Agile is faster” as evidence
- Listing many templates without explaining the decision each template supports
- Confusing Hotel Booking launch with the 18-month direct booking target

## Remediation Guidance

If the learner confuses PMBOK and methodology, send them back to “PMBOK คืออะไร” and “PMBOK ไม่ใช่อะไร” in the learner chapter.

If the learner confuses output and outcome, ask them to rewrite one scenario as four lines: output, outcome, benefit, value.

If the learner has no blind spots, ask them to state their professional bias first: technical, stakeholder, schedule, finance, operations or product. Then ask what that bias tends to hide.

If the learner chooses Agile/Predictive/Hybrid without evidence, require owner, inputs, trade-off and review trigger before scoring.

## Facilitation Notes

Keep the tone respectful when learners bring strong prior experience. The point is not to invalidate experience, but to show how shared professional language makes experience transferable and auditable.

Avoid introducing detailed Process Groups or Knowledge Areas here. Lesson 01 should create appetite and mental model; Lesson 02 handles project/operation boundaries, and later lessons expand the framework.

## Artifact Approval Guidance

Approve the PM Learning Baseline at `Usable` when it includes:

- A real or scenario-based project context
- Distinct output, outcome, benefit and value assumptions
- At least two blind spots
- At least four evidence gaps or validation questions
- Clear artifact governance: creator, owner, reviewer, approval authority and review evidence

Mark it `Professional` when assumptions are tied to scenario evidence, decision risk and the next validation action in Lesson 02.
