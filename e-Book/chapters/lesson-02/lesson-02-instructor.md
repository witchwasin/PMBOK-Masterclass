## Thinking Walkthrough

### Step 1: Start from Business Need

Do not let learners begin with “ERP is the answer.” Start with the business need: close-cycle delay, inconsistent data and need for reliable information.

### Step 2: Classify by Nature of Work

Monthly close is Operation because it repeats and sustains the business. ERP design/build/migration/UAT/cutover is Project because it creates a unique organizational capability. A temporary report fix may be operational improvement.

### Step 3: Identify Transition Point

Ask when accountability moves from project team to operation. Hypercare may be transitional. Steady support should have an IT Operations Owner and Finance Process Owner.

### Step 4: Force Measure and Owner

Every Value Chain stage needs measure/evidence and accountable owner. Without this, learners will stop at “system delivered.”

### Step 5: Connect to Governance

Classification determines governance. A quick operational fix needs a different owner, approval route and measure from a full ERP transformation.

## Completed Example

### Classification

| Work | Classification | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly financial close | Operation | CFO / Finance Process Owner |
| Temporary report correction | Operational improvement | Finance Systems Owner |
| ERP design, build, migration, UAT and cutover | Project | Sponsor and Project Manager |
| Hypercare then steady-state support | Transitional work to Operation | PM, then IT Operations Owner |

### Value Chain

| Stage | Statement | Evidence | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Business Need | ข้อมูลไม่ตรงและปิดงบ 15 วัน | Current close calendar | CFO |
| Output | ERP 5 modules, migrated data, trained users | Acceptance records | PM |
| Outcome | ใช้ process/data กลาง | Adoption and data-quality metrics | Process Owners |
| Benefit | ปิดงบภายใน 5 working days | Six-month benefits review | CFO |
| Business Value | ตัดสินใจเร็วขึ้นและรองรับการเติบโต | Executive KPI review | Executive Sponsor |
| Transition | Hypercare, support model and owner acceptance | Handover record | PM and IT Operations Owner |

## Model Answer

Monthly close is Operation even though it has a deadline. ERP Transformation is Project because it is a temporary initiative that creates a unique business capability. A temporary report correction can be operational improvement and may run alongside ERP when owner and scope are separated.

The correct Value Chain does not stop at go-live. It connects ERP output to adoption, data quality and the target of reducing close cycle from 15 days to 5 working days. CFO or Finance Process Owner should own the benefit after handover.

Evidence to ask for:

- root cause of close delay
- cost of delay
- current adoption/data baseline
- key-user capacity and backfill
- transition owner
- date when benefit must be visible

Recommendations other than the completed example are acceptable when they show evidence, trade-off and do not claim that a solution creates value automatically.

## Review Checklist

- [ ] Classification does not rely only on deadline
- [ ] Temporary, unique and ongoing work are separated
- [ ] Transitional work and Operation Owner are visible
- [ ] Value Chain covers Need, Output, Outcome, Benefit and Value
- [ ] Every stage has measure/evidence and owner
- [ ] Missing information appears before recommendation
- [ ] ERP working budget and total funding envelope are not confused
- [ ] Hotel Booking target, budget and timeline remain source-accurate
- [ ] Recommendation includes governance and next decision

## Scoring Rubric

| Dimension | Incomplete | Usable | Professional |
|---|---|---|---|
| Classification | Uses deadline or size only | Uses temporary/unique/ongoing | Separates transitional work and governance consequence |
| Value Chain | Stops at output | Reaches benefit/value | Adds baseline, evidence and review date |
| Ownership | No owner | Sponsor/Operation Owner named | Creator, benefit owner, reviewer and approver are distinct |
| Evidence | Guesses | Names gaps | Prioritizes evidence and decision deadline |
| Recommendation | No trade-off | Selects option with reason | Shows options, risk and next action |
| Scenario Accuracy | Contradicts facts | Uses key facts correctly | Preserves all locked facts and labels assumptions |

Passing level: every dimension at least Usable.

## Debrief Questions

- Which row of your classification table would change governance the most
- Where is the transition point
- Which owner owns output, and which owner owns benefit
- What evidence would make a short-term report fix sufficient
- What evidence proves Hotel Booking value after launch

## Common Learner Errors

- Calling monthly close a Project because it has a deadline
- Treating go-live as outcome
- Listing value without measure or owner
- Assigning all post-handover benefits to PM
- Ignoring matrix authority and key-user capacity
- Calling every post-launch issue a new project

## Remediation Guidance

If classification is weak, ask the learner to mark each work item as temporary/unique or ongoing/repetitive before choosing a governance route.

If value chain stops at output, ask “what behavior or business performance changes after this output is used?”

If ownership is missing, require three owners: sponsor/business owner, project delivery owner and operation owner.

If the learner overclaims Hotel Booking success, ask them to separate launch evidence from 18-month direct booking target evidence.

## Facilitation Notes

Do not teach the full 5 Process Groups here. Mention that Lesson 03 will show how initiating, planning, executing, monitoring/controlling and closing manage the project boundary identified in this chapter.

When learners ask whether operational improvement can be a project, answer through context: a one-time cross-functional improvement may be managed as a project, while recurring work remains operation.

## Artifact Approval Guidance

Approve the artifact at `Usable` when it:

- classifies at least four work items
- includes transition point and operation owner
- has Value Chain through Benefit and Business Value
- names measure/evidence and accountable owner for every stage
- identifies missing information before recommendation
- proposes governance and next decision

Mark `Professional` when the answer also shows trade-offs, risk of misclassification, decision deadline and clean handoff to Lesson 03.
