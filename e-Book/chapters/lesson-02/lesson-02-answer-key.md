## A. Concept Check

Expected answers:

- Project is a temporary effort that creates a unique product, service, result or change
- Closing monthly financial accounts is usually Operation
- Go-live is an output milestone that still needs adoption and operation readiness
- Direct booking increasing from 10% to 35% is an outcome/value indicator
- In a functional organization, PM needs influence, negotiation and escalation paths
- Delivery status and outcome/adoption status should be reported separately
- A tested ERP finance module is output
- Daily booking support after go-live is Operation
- Organization structure affects PM authority, resource availability and escalation
- Number of documents produced is a weak success measure

## B. ERP Decision Case

A strong answer frames the CFO request as portfolio-level or combined governance because it may require both short-term finance improvement and longer ERP capability build.

Key distinctions:

- Monthly close: Operation
- Temporary report correction: operational improvement
- ERP design/build/migration/UAT/cutover: Project
- Hypercare/support transition: transitional work into Operation

Missing evidence:

- root cause of the 15-day close
- cost of delay
- finance resource capacity
- master-data ownership
- key-user availability
- adoption/data baseline
- benefit review date

Outcome ownership should sit with CFO or Finance Process Owner. PM owns analysis, delivery coordination and decision evidence, not the permanent business benefit alone.

Recommended next action: prepare one-page option brief for Steering Committee comparing quick fix, ERP-only and combined approach, including owner, risk, cost of delay and transition implications.

## C. Hotel Booking Decision Case

Delivered outputs:

- web booking channel
- mobile app
- landing page
- payment integration
- back office tools
- PMS/inventory integration capability if delivered

Lagging outcomes:

- direct booking is only 14% after one month
- payment failure is 8%
- hotel partners update inventory late
- customer conversion and partner adoption are at risk

Operation issues include daily booking support, incident handling and routine inventory updates. Payment stabilization or partner inventory behavior may become an improvement initiative or follow-on project if it requires temporary cross-functional change.

Sponsor report should split delivery from outcome: delivery completed, outcome at risk, stabilization plan required, named owners for conversion, payment reliability and partner adoption.

## D. Artifact Construction

Minimum usable artifact includes:

- decision context with scenario, business need, baseline and decision deadline
- classification table using Project / Operation / Transitional logic
- Value Chain through Business Value and Transition to Operation
- measure/evidence and accountable owner for every stage
- missing information and risk if misclassified
- governance recommendation and next decision
- approval fields

Professional artifacts include option trade-offs, risk of misclassification, benefit review date and clear Lesson 03 handoff.

## E. Artifact Review

Weak classification: “This has a deadline and large budget, so it is a project.”

Correction should use:

- temporary and unique
- ongoing and repetitive
- transition point
- owner
- governance consequence
- measure/evidence

Good review language: “The deadline shows urgency, not project nature. We need to classify the work by whether it creates a unique change or sustains recurring operation.”

## F. Reflection / Executive Brief

A strong five-sentence brief includes:

1. Business need
2. Output being delivered
3. Outcome or benefit expected
4. Owner after transition
5. Next decision requested

It should not say value is achieved simply because output is delivered.
