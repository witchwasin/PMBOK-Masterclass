# Lesson 01 Assessment — Why Project Managers Must Know PMBOK

> ทำ [PM Learning Baseline](learner/Artifact-Template.md) ก่อนเปิด [Model Answer](instructor/Model-Answer.md). ใช้ [Scoring Rubric](instructor/Scoring-Rubric.md) ตัดสินระดับ Incomplete, Usable หรือ Professional.

> At least 80% of marks assess application and judgement, not recall.

## 1. Decision Case — Tailoring Before a Project Starts (35 points)

The CEO asks the ERP team to “use Agile because it is faster.” The project has five ERP modules, six legacy systems to migrate, regulatory finance requirements, a fixed go-live before 1 October, and 80 key users who are not fully dedicated. The team has not yet agreed what “Agile” would change.

1. Write the five most important questions you would ask before choosing the delivery approach. (10)
2. Recommend a starting approach—predictive, agile, or hybrid—and state two assumptions that could change your recommendation. (10)
3. State the decision owner, evidence required, one trade-off, and one review trigger. (10)
4. Explain why “Agile is faster” is not sufficient evidence. (5)

**Answer direction:** A strong answer distinguishes compliance/scope stability from discovery work; it does not declare one approach universally superior.
**Warning signs:** Treating PMBOK as a mandatory method; selecting a method without decision authority or evidence.
**Source classification:** `[Teaching Scenario]` with `[Professional Opinion]` for the recommendation framework.

## 2. Artifact Review — Output Is Not Yet Value (30 points)

Review this draft closure statement:

> “The Hotel Booking mobile app and website launched on time and within the 12 ล้านบาท budget. The project is successful.”

1. Identify four claims or missing measures that prevent this from proving success. (12)
2. Rewrite the statement to separate output, outcome, benefit, and business value. (12)
3. Name the role that should own the post-launch benefit measure and a review date. (6)

**Answer direction:** The output is the launched product. Outcomes include adoption and completed bookings; benefits include reduced OTA commission; value needs business evidence over time.
**Warning signs:** Calling launch itself an outcome; assigning all benefits to the PM after handover.
**Source classification:** `[PMBOK]` for value orientation; `[Teaching Scenario]` for the 12 ล้านบาท baseline.

## 3. Ambiguous Scenario — Experience and Framework (25 points)

A senior PM says: “I have delivered ERP for 15 years, so I do not need PMBOK. Templates slow me down.”

Write a respectful response of no more than 180 words. It must explain what professional experience contributes, what a shared framework contributes, and how tailoring prevents unnecessary process.

**Evaluation:** balanced reasoning (10), distinction between framework and checklist (8), clear tailoring logic (7).
**Warning signs:** Dismissing experience or claiming PMBOK guarantees success.
**Source classification:** `[PMBOK]` and `[Professional Opinion]`.

## 4. Retrieval Check (10 points)

Classify each item as **output**, **outcome**, **benefit**, or **business value** and give one reason:

1. ERP users record finance transactions in the new system.
2. The organisation closes monthly accounts in five days rather than fifteen.
3. A unified ERP system is deployed.
4. Leaders make better inventory decisions from timely data.

**Source classification:** `[Teaching Scenario]` using ERP Scenario Master v1.0.
